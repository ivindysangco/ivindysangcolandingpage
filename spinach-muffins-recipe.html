Healthy Spinach Muffins with Yogurt

No Banana Version - Protein-Packed & Nutritious

Prep Time 10 min
Cook Time 20 min
Total Time 30 min
Difficulty Beginner

Ingredients

Wet Ingredients (blend in food processor/blender):

  • 2 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt (whole fat preferred)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4-1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Dry Ingredients:

  • 1 3/4 cups white whole wheat flour (or all-purpose flour)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Optional Add-ins:

  • Chocolate chips
  • Blueberries
  • Raisins

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a muffin tin or use muffin liners.
  2. Blend wet ingredients: Add spinach, yogurt, eggs, milk, honey/maple syrup, and vanilla to a blender and blend until completely smooth and bright green.
  3. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Combine: Pour the wet green mixture into the dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined. Don't overmix - a few lumps are okay!
  5. Add optional mix-ins: If using chocolate chips, blueberries, or raisins, gently fold them in now.
  6. Fill muffin cups: Divide batter into muffin cups, filling about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake: Bake for 18-20 minutes (or 12-14 minutes for mini muffins) until a toothpick comes out clean and tops are lightly golden.
  8. Cool and enjoy: Let cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

Important Notes & Tips

  • Sweetness: Since yogurt isn't as sweet as banana, you'll need more sweetener (honey or maple syrup) to compensate. Start with 1/4 cup and taste the batter - add more if needed.
  • Moisture: The muffins will still be moist and fluffy thanks to the Greek yogurt, which also adds extra protein.
  • Color: Don't be alarmed by the bright green color - it's normal! The spinach flavor is very mild.
  • Storage: Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3-4 days.
  • Freezing: These freeze beautifully! Freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or microwave for 30-45 seconds.
  • Meal Prep: Perfect for making ahead. Double the batch and freeze half for quick breakfasts.

Nutrition Highlights

These muffins are packed with nutrients from spinach (iron, vitamins A & K) and Greek yogurt (protein, calcium). Using whole wheat flour adds fiber, while the natural sweeteners keep them healthier than traditional muffins. Perfect for getting more vegetables into your diet in a delicious way!
